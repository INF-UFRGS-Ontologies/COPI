1 Identity-Giving FunctionFunção Identitária
Function class
SubsurfaceResourceInteractionFunction ⊑ DesignedFunction
Realized in
SubsurfaceResourceInteractionProcess ⊑ PlannedProcess
The identity-giving function encompasses the full scope of well operations: obtaining/producing resources from the earth, or storing/injecting materials into the earth. This is what distinguishes a well from other equipment - its designed purpose to interact with subsurface formations and resources.
2 Genus DeterminationDeterminação do Gênero
EngineeredSystembfo:ObjectAggregate (BFO_0000027)
A well is fundamentally a collection of interacting components (wellbore, casing, completion equipment, surface facilities) that work together but lack a single causal boundary. The CFIHOS definition explicitly states 'collection of holes' and 'services related to production', indicating an aggregate system rather than a single unified object. Wells span from surface to subsurface with multiple distinct subsystems.
3 Differentia SpecificaDiferença Específica
∃bearerOf.SubsurfaceResourceInteractionFunction

An engineered system that interacts with subsurface resources through a drilled wellbore penetrating the earth.

The specific function of subsurface resource interaction combined with the physical requirement of a wellbore are the minimal conditions that distinguish wells from other engineered systems. The wellbore is the essential physical component that enables the subsurface interaction function.
4 Necessary PartsPartes Necessárias

No necessary parts recorded.

The wellbore is the only universal component - all other parts (casing, completion equipment, surface facilities) vary significantly by well type, purpose, and completion design. Used hasComponentPartAtSomeTime because wells may be temporarily without surface equipment during drilling or workover operations.

PPDM Well
A Well is a permitted or actual drilled hole in the ground designed to exchange (or facilitate the exchange of) fluids between a subsurface reservoir and the surface (or another reservoir), or to enable the detection and measurement of rock properties. Key concept: A physical Well is created each time the drill bit breaks the surface of the earth. Clarification: A Well is the parent of the Wellhead Stream(s), Well Origin, and all Well Components down hole from its Well Origin. A Well has a single Well Origin and all Wellbore(s) that extend from this Well Origin are parts of the Well. Re-entry through the same Well Origin for a Well that has not been plugged and abandoned does not create a new Well. A Well that has the same geographic coordinates as a previously existing plugged and abandoned, reclaimed or undrilled Well is a new Well.
