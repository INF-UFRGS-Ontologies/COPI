A knock out drum is a separator designed to separate relatively small quantities of liquid from a vapor stream through gravitational and momentum-based phase separation.

The identity-giving function is the specific type of separation that removes relatively small quantities of liquid from a vapor stream. This is a specialized subtype of general SeparationFunction, distinguished by the vapor-to-liquid phase separation with minimal liquid content expected.
2 Genus DeterminationDeterminação do Gênero
Separatorbfo:Object (BFO_0000030)
All source definitions consistently position knock out drum as a specialized type of separator. The enriched Separator class (function: SeparationFunction, genus: Vessel) is the most specific appropriate superclass. While ISO 15926 also lists VESSEL, Separator is more specific and captures the separative nature that distinguishes this from general containment vessels.
3 Differentia SpecificaDiferença Específica
∃bearerOf.KnockOutSeparationFunction

Designed specifically to separate relatively small quantities of liquid from a vapor stream, as opposed to other separation functions like three-phase separation or general liquid-liquid separation.

The differentia is the specialized KnockOutSeparationFunction that distinguishes this from other separator types. The 'relatively small quantities' qualifier in all definitions indicates this is not for bulk liquid-vapor separation but for removing entrained liquid from predominantly vapor streams.
